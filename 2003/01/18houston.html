1:05pm CST Saturday 18 January 2003

Leaving Janette's now for Houston. How amazing that the shortest trip I've taken can feel like the longest of all.

2:38pm

93 miles traveled.

This drive feels like it's taking forever. Is it because I've seen it all before? If so, that violates my belief that suggests when a long drive is made repetitively, that it seems to take a shorter time after a while. This could be described as "getting used to it," but I think it's more precisely that the brain disengages and just goes on auto pilot for the journey, making the time seem to pass more quickly.

I've got Eminem blasting, and I'm just flat driving, which seems to be just what I did on my driving treks. Maybe this is different because I have an agenda; I'm specifically driving to Houston, and am not willing to stop anywhere along the way. Then I'm all, "oh a stop won't hurt" and then I'm all, "but at least get half way there."

And that's just the thing; I'm not even halfway there, but I'm still all ugggg that this trip is already taking forever.

4:49pm

Have basically made it to Maggie's. At the halfway point (123 miles) I found some sunflower seeds in the magic box Janette had given me so long ago. Yay Janette!
